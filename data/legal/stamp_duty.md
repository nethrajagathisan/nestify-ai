# Stamp Duty and Registration Charges in India

When you buy a property in India, stamp duty and registration charges are mandatory state levies paid to legally transfer ownership. These are calculated as a percentage of the property's market value or agreement value, whichever is higher.

## Stamp Duty Rates by State

| State | Male | Female | Joint Ownership |
|-------|------|--------|-----------------|
| Karnataka | 5% | 5% | 5% |
| Tamil Nadu | 7% | 7% | 7% |
| Telangana | 5% | 5% | 5% |
| Maharashtra | 5%* | 4%* | 5%* |
| Delhi | 6% | 4% | 5% |
| Punjab | 7% | 5% | 6% |

*Maharashtra charges an additional 1% metro cess in Mumbai, Thane, Navi Mumbai, and Pune, making effective rates 6% for men and 5% for women in these areas.

## Registration Charges

Registration charges are typically **1% of the property value** across most states, subject to a maximum cap in some regions:

- Karnataka: 1% (capped at Rs. 50,000 in some urban areas)
- Tamil Nadu: 1% (no cap)
- Telangana: 0.5% to 1%
- Maharashtra: 1% (capped at Rs. 30,000)
- Delhi: 1% (capped at Rs. 15,000 for male, Rs. 10,000 for female)
- Punjab: 1%

## Special Concessions

- **Women buyers**: Several states offer 1-2% lower stamp duty to encourage property ownership by women.
- **Affordable housing**: Some states provide partial waivers or reduced rates for properties under affordable housing schemes.
- **Gift deeds**: Stamp duty for gifting to close relatives is often lower (e.g., 2-3% in Karnataka).

## Example Calculation

For a property valued at **Rs. 80 lakhs** in **Maharashtra** (female buyer, Mumbai):
- Stamp duty: 80,00,000 x 5% = Rs. 4,00,000
- Metro cess: 80,00,000 x 1% = Rs. 80,000
- Registration: Rs. 30,000 (capped)
- **Total**: Rs. 4,00,000 + Rs. 80,000 + Rs. 30,000 = **Rs. 4,90,000**

Always verify current rates on the respective state registration department website before budgeting.
