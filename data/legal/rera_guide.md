# RERA Guide for Home Buyers in India

## What is RERA?

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) is a landmark legislation that brought transparency and accountability to India's real estate sector. Before RERA, buyers often faced delays, misleading advertisements, and fund diversion by developers. RERA mandates that all real estate projects and agents must register with their respective state regulatory authority.

## Why RERA Matters

RERA ensures:
- **Project delays are penalized**: Developers must deliver on promised timelines or pay interest to buyers.
- **Fund diversion is prohibited**: 70% of project funds must be kept in a dedicated escrow account and used only for that project.
- **Standardized carpet area**: Pricing must be based on actual carpet area, not ambiguous terms like "super built-up area."
- **Transparent advertising**: Any claim made in marketing material must reflect in the registered project details.

## How to Check Builder Registration

Every state has its own RERA portal. To verify a builder:

1. Visit the state RERA website (e.g., [rera.karnataka.gov.in](https://rera.karnataka.gov.in), [maharera.mahaonline.gov.in](https://maharera.mahaonline.gov.in)).
2. Search using the project name, builder name, or RERA registration number.
3. Review project details: approved layout, timeline, land title status, and builder track record.
4. Cross-check the RERA number on project brochures and advertisements.

If a project is not registered, avoid booking until registration is complete.

## Common Buyer Complaints and Resolution

Buyers frequently file complaints for:
- **Delay in possession** — the most common grievance.
- **Deviation from approved plans** — changes in layout or amenities.
- **False advertising** — amenities promised but not delivered.
- **Title defects** — legal disputes over land ownership.

### Resolution Process

1. **File a complaint** with the state RERA authority online or in person. The fee is typically low (around Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000).
2. **RERA Tribunal** examines evidence from both parties.
3. **Order** is usually issued within 60 days.
4. **Appeal** can be filed with the Appellate Tribunal if either party disagrees.
5. **Enforcement** — RERA can impose penalties, cancel registrations, or direct compensation.

## Key Buyer Protections Under RERA

- **Right to information**: Buyers can access all project documents, approvals, and progress updates.
- **Compensation for delay**: If possession is delayed, developers pay interest at SBI's MCLR + 2%.
- **Defect liability**: Developers are liable for structural defects for 5 years from possession.
- **No arbitrary changes**: Developers cannot alter plans without written consent from two-thirds of buyers.
- **Agent accountability**: Real estate agents must also register and can be penalized for misrepresentation.

RERA has significantly shifted power toward buyers, making it essential to verify registration before any payment.
