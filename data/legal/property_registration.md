# Property Registration Process in India

Registering a property sale is a legal requirement under the Registration Act, 1908. An unregistered sale deed is not admissible as evidence in court and does not transfer valid title.

## Sale Deed Process

The transfer of property from seller to buyer follows these steps:

1. **Draft the sale deed**: Engage a lawyer to draft the deed with accurate property details, consideration amount, and parties' information.
2. **Stamp duty payment**: Pay stamp duty through e-stamping or physical stamp paper (varies by state; see stamp_duty.md).
3. **Registration appointment**: Book a slot at the Sub-Registrar's office where the property is located.
4. **Biometric verification**: Both seller and buyer (or their registered Power of Attorney holders) must appear for photo and fingerprint verification.
5. **Document execution**: Sign the deed before the Sub-Registrar.
6. **Registration fee payment**: Pay registration charges (typically 1% of property value).
7. **Receipt of registered deed**: Collect the registered document, usually within a few days.

## Encumbrance Certificate (EC)

An **Encumbrance Certificate** is a critical document that reveals all registered transactions on a property for a specified period (usually 13-30 years).

- **Why it is crucial**: It confirms whether the property has any existing mortgage, lien, or legal claim.
- **How to obtain**: Apply at the Sub-Registrar's office or through state online portals.
- **What to check**: Ensure there are no gaps in the EC history and no undisclosed loans.
- **Red flags**: Unexplained transactions, missing years, or existing encumbrances not disclosed by the seller.

## Khata and Mutation

- **Khata** (in Karnataka) or **Patta** (in Tamil Nadu) is the property account maintained by local municipal authorities. It records the owner for tax purposes.
- **Mutation** (also called **Dakhil Kharij**) is the process of updating revenue records to reflect the new owner's name after purchase.
- **Why it matters**: Without mutation, you remain liable for previous owner's dues and cannot sell or transfer the property easily.
- **Timeline**: Apply within 3-6 months of registration. Processing time varies by municipality (30-90 days).

## TDS on Property Purchase

Under Section 194-IA of the Income Tax Act:

- **Applicability**: Buyer must deduct TDS at **1%** if the property value exceeds **Rs. 50 lakhs**.
- **Responsibility**: It is the **buyer's** duty to deduct and deposit TDS, not the seller's.
- **Form 26QB**: Submit online through the TIN NSDL portal within **30 days** of the month of deduction.
- **TDS Certificate**: Issue Form 16B to the seller, which they claim while filing returns.
- **PAN requirement**: Both buyer and seller must have valid PAN cards.

## Timeline for Registration

| Step | Typical Duration |
|------|------------------|
| Drafting sale deed | 3-5 days |
| Stamp duty payment | Same day (e-stamping) |
| Sub-Registrar appointment | 1-7 days |
| Registration execution | 1 day |
| Document collection | 3-15 days |
| Mutation application | 30-90 days |

**Total time**: Typically **1 to 3 months** from agreement to complete record update.

Always insist on registering the sale deed promptly. Possession without registration does not confer legal ownership.
