# NRI Property Purchase Guide for India

Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs) are permitted to buy immovable property in India under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999. However, specific rules govern the type of property, funding source, and repatriation.

## FEMA Rules for NRI Property Purchase

- **No prior RBI permission** is required for NRIs to purchase residential or commercial property.
- **Payment must be made** through normal banking channels from abroad or from an NRE/NRO/FCNR account.
- **Foreign currency** can be remitted for purchase, but the transaction must be documented.
- **Gift and inheritance**: NRIs can inherit property from Indian residents or other NRIs. Gifting to relatives is allowed within FEMA limits.

## Types of Property NRIs Can Buy

| Property Type | Allowed | Notes |
|---------------|---------|-------|
| Residential | Yes | No restrictions on number of units |
| Commercial | Yes | Suitable for office or retail investment |
| Agricultural land | **No** | Cannot purchase farmland, plantation, or farmhouse |
| Inherited agricultural | Yes | Allowed through inheritance only |

NRIs seeking agricultural property must acquire it only through inheritance or as a gift from a resident Indian who originally purchased it under permissible rules.

## NRO vs NRE Accounts

| Feature | NRE Account | NRO Account |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Currency held | Indian Rupees | Indian Rupees |
| Source of funds | Foreign earnings only | Indian income (rent, dividends, salary) |
| Repatriability | Fully repatriable | Limited ($1 million per year with Form 15CA/CB) |
| Taxation in India | Tax-free interest | Interest taxable |
| Joint holding | With NRI/PIO only | With resident Indian allowed |

For property purchase, funds can come from either account. However, rental income must be credited to an NRO account.

## Repatriation Limits

- **Sale proceeds**: Up to **two residential properties** can have sale proceeds fully repatriated.
- **Annual cap**: Repatriation from NRO account is capped at **USD 1 million per financial year** (April to March).
- **Conditions**: The property must have been purchased in accordance with FEMA rules, and applicable taxes must be paid in India.
- **Form 15CA/CB**: Required for repatriation exceeding certain thresholds; a chartered accountant must certify Form 15CB.

## Reporting to RBI

While routine purchases do not require individual RBI approval, compliance reporting is essential:

- **E-form submission**: Certain high-value transactions or repatriations require reporting through authorized dealer banks.
- **Form 15CA/CB**: Mandatory for remittances above prescribed limits; the CA certifies that taxes have been properly deducted or paid.
- **Document retention**: Maintain sale deed, PAN, passport copy, and FEMA declaration for at least 5 years.

## Practical Checklist for NRI Buyers

1. Verify property type is permissible (residential/commercial only for direct purchase).
2. Ensure funds come from NRE/NRO/FCNR or foreign remittance.
3. Obtain a Power of Attorney (PoA) in India if you cannot be present for registration.
4. Check builder RERA registration and project approvals.
5. Deduct TDS at 1% if property value exceeds Rs. 50 lakhs (buyer's responsibility).
6. File Form 15CA/CB for repatriation of rental income or sale proceeds.
7. Keep all FEMA compliance documents for future reference.

NRIs should consult a chartered accountant and legal advisor familiar with FEMA before large transactions to avoid compliance issues.
